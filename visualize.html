Understanding RoPE

Rotary Positional Embeddings — An Interactive Guide

Step 1 of 5
The Permutation Problem
Raw self attention has no concept of word order, it treats a sentence as an unordered set of tokens. Drag dog and man to swap them and watch what happens to the attention matrix.
Word order:
bit ↔ dog attention score: 0.82
QKT Attention Score Matrix  —  ★ = "bit" ↔ "dog" score

After swapping, the matrix rows and columns shuffle to reflect the new token order, but the 0.82 score between "bit" and "dog" never changes. Standard attention is purely content based; it has no way to distinguish "The dog bit the man" from "The man bit the dog."

Step 2 of 5
The "Absolute Addition" Flaw
The original Transformer adds a sinusoidal position vector directly to the word embedding. Drag the slider to see how this corrupts "dog"'s semantic location in embedding space.
Semantic embedding:"dog" (fixed)
PEx = sin(pos / 100000.45): 0.000
PEy = cos(pos / 100000.56): 1.000
Combined |PE|:0.000
Displacement from pos=0:0.000
Semantic Distortion: None

The same word "dog" ends up at a different point in embedding space depending on its position. Two identical words at different positions look semantically different to the model, addition corrupts content with location.

Step 3 of 5
The Rotary Mechanism
Instead of adding a position vector, RoPE rotates the embedding vector by an angle proportional to its position. The vector's length, its semantic magnitude, never changes.
Rotation angle (mθ):0.0°
Vector x:1.000
Vector y:0.000
Vector magnitude: 1.000 ✓

No matter what position you set, the magnitude stays exactly 1.000. Position is encoded as a direction, not a magnitude shift, the word's semantic strength is geometrically intact. The faint trails show every previous angle the vector has swept through.

Step 4 of 5
The Relative Distance Payoff
Absolute rotations yield relative distances automatically when you compute the attention dot product. Shift both tokens forward — absolute angles change, dot product stays constant.
Base angle (θ):22.5°
Angle A (mθ):22.5°
Angle B (nθ):67.5°
Relative diff (n−m)θ:45.0°
Dot Product: 0.707

Hit "Shift Both Forward +1" repeatedly. The individual angles keep growing, but the dot product never changes because cos((n−m)θ) depends only on the gap between tokens, not their absolute positions. This is RoPE's guarantee: attention scores encode relative, not absolute, positions.

Step 5 of 5
Where & How RoPE Is Applied
Position is not injected at the start. Word embeddings stay purely semantic. RoPE is applied inside every attention layer — and only to the Query and Key tensors, never the Values.

Because only Q and K are rotated, the dot product Q·KT encodes the relative angle between positions — giving the model position aware attention scores. V is never rotated: it carries raw content, not position. This is why RoPE generalises to longer sequences than those seen during training.
